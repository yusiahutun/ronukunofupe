The 11 Best Dry Shampoos for Fine Hair of 2024, Tested

It’s a familiar tug-of-war for anyone with fine hair — the days where we’re simply not up for a full hair wash, yet our greasy roots and flat hair don’t exactly look clean. But instead of rocking a sad slick-back bun, you have other styling options. A dry shampoo absorbs oil and can prevent a lifeless look by giving roots a much-needed volume boost, especially on a non-wash day. “As your hair becomes dirtier in-between shampoos, it’s important to use a dry shampoo with volumizing benefits that will make your hair look like it was just washed and a style that can get you through another day,” explains celebrity hairstylist Christina Giarrizzo.

If you have fine hair and your current dry shampoo just isn’t cutting it, our team of InStyle editors — all with fine hair — tested more than 60 dry shampoos. As we integrated each dry shampoo into our normal hair care routines for two weeks, we paid close attention to how fast and thoroughly it absorbed oil and neutralized odor, any lingering white cast or sticky residue it left behind, if it was easy to use or if it made a mess of our bathrooms, and most importantly, the final result. Did we like the way our hair looked? Did our roots still appear greasy or flat? These are the dry shampoos that we loved the most for the way they gave our (formerly) dirty, fine hair a volumized, full and bouncy looking, fresh start.

Best Overall

Crown Affair The Dry Shampoo

Sephora

What We Love: This powder formula uses a blend of super finely milled starches to absorb oil instantly. 

What We Don’t Love: Those who use dry shampoo more often than regular shampoo may find that this is a small amount of product.

Not only do we love a dry shampoo that effortlessly soaks up excess oil at your roots, but it’s even better when the product is mess-free, without leaving a white cast or sticky residue behind, with a gentle, pleasant aroma. And this luxe powder formula checked all these boxes. 

Our straight, fine hair tends to show signs of shine and oil within 24 hours of washing, and we needed a dry shampoo that felt light on our scalp, but still neutralized oiliness and odors effectively. We loved using this ultra-fine powder that dissolved into our hair without feeling chalky or creating build-up on our scalp. A little goes a long way, and with the accompanying brush, you can literally paint the powder onto your roots with total precision and control, not to mention, ensuring a mess-free application. Plus, we were obsessed with the light citrusy, floral scent that wafted from the inclusion of persimmon in the formula, which acts as a natural deodorizer with anti-inflammatory properties — perfect for scalp health. 

Type: Powder | Scented: Yes | Size: 0.53 oz 

Best Dry Shampoo for Thin Fine Hair

Act + Acre Volumizing Dry Shampoo

Sephora

What We Love: Fulvic acid in this formula helps keep scalp health in check while also soaking up oil and odor. 

What We Don’t Love: Although the bottle is small, you don’t need much to get major results.

If you have dry and damaged locks, using a dry shampoo developed with ingredients to keep the scalp nourished and healthy is essential. We found that the clean, all-natural plant-based dry shampoo from Act+Acre was gentle enough for our scalp, our dry strands, and highlighted hair — a tall order! It helped our damage-prone tresses not only maintain a lush, full look for another day or two, but the formula's antioxidant-rich fulvic acid also provides antimicrobial properties, which helped keep our reactive scalp and skin soothed without a hint of irritation.

With a blend of oil- and odor-absorbing tapioca powder and rice starch, our greasy roots were eliminated instantly, giving our hair a much cleaner look and feel. It was also a breeze to target exactly where we wanted to mattify our hair, as each puff comes from the pump's small, pointed opening to release an ultra-concentrated amount of product directly into our hair and nowhere else. Because of this thoughtful design, we only needed a few puffs to bring our hair back to life. Even better, this 0.6-ounce bottle is perfect for travel.

Type: Powder | Scented: No | Size: 0.6 oz

Best Dry Shampoo for Fine Oily Hair

Briogeo Scalp Revival Dry Shampoo

Ulta

What We Love: It actually cleans hair and stimulates new hair growth.

What We Don’t Love: We wish it provided a little more volume for our fine strands.  

Briogeo is one of our favorite haircare brands for their thoughtful, health-first formulas, and this clean dry shampoo is just another example. It’s lightweight, super absorbent, easy to use, and even though we don’t love a fragrance in a dry shampoo, we didn’t mind this one because it just smelled clean. 

With a combination of natural ingredients, like kaolin clay, and tapioca, and rice starches, this airy dry shampoo soaked up the excess oil and grime from our roots and blended in right away with minimal effort — a true first for our jet-black hair. It also contains witch hazel, a natural astringent known for curbing oil production, and detoxifying binchotan charcoal to keep the scalp free of build-up, along with strand-strengthening biotin to protect future hair growth. (We didn’t notice stronger hair strands within the two-week testing period, but we’ve continued to use it and are excited to see if it delivers on this promise.)

Type: Powder | Scent: Yes | Size: 1.7 oz

Best Dry Shampoo Foam for Fine Hair

InnerSense Organic Refresh Dry Shampoo

Amazon

What We Love: It’s rare for a dry shampoo to actually cleanse the hair, but this one does.

What We Don’t Love: Since it's a wet formula, we had to blow out our hair after every application, so it may not really save you much time.

If you struggle with a dry scalp, the last thing you need is a dry shampoo that makes it worse and flaky. If this sounds a bit like your hair type, consider the Refresh Dry Shampoo from Innersense. After just one use, our scalps actually felt nourished — a rare feat for a product like a dry shampoo (which doesn’t typically target dryness). Although we were nervous about using a wet foam-to-powder formula, it kept our scalps soothed and hydrated, without causing our hair to dry into piecey strands. Witch hazel is the hero ingredient in this formula, as it works as a natural astringent to control oil production, while also ridding the scalp of bacteria.

We did find that the foam-to-powder texture took some getting used to, but once we got the routine down, we loved the result. After massaging the foam into our hair, we'd use a blow dryer on our wet hair to finish styling, leaving us with hair that looked just as clean as if we actually just showered. Plus, the hair dryer gave us a bit more body, which is never a bad thing in our book. Ultimately, we didn’t mind the extra effort, given that this formula prevented our fine hair from looking dull and flat and it even made our hair feel softer, too. It does have a scent, but the light citrus leans clean and fresh, not cheesy tropical.

Type: Foam to powder | Scent: Yes | Size: 2.37 oz

Best Powder Dry Shampoo for Fine Hair

Odele Dry Shampoo

Odele

What We Love: The nozzle makes it easy to concentrate this formula to the areas of your roots that need the most help with oil absorption. 

What We Don’t Love: It's white and will need to be rubbed into dark hair. 

While we can understand why a powder formula may seem intimidating for typical aerosol users at first, the one from Odele makes the process seamless and perhaps even simpler than using a spray. We loved this option for how easy it was to use, especially when we wanted to pinpoint a specific oily area. One of our editors found this option to be especially great on her bangs, which tend to get oily fast, even on days when she actually has washed her hair. The nozzle allowed her to carefully control the amount of powder that released into her fine bangs (because overdoing it would make them look heavy), as well as her roots. Plus, the light little puffs that are released are cute. 

With corn and rice starch, arrowroot powder, and kaolin, it absorbed all the grime easily, and also gave our roots a visible lift.

Type: Powder | Scent: Yes | Size: 1.13 oz

What really won us over was the striking volume this oil-free powder gave us. Its concentrated nozzle allowed us to hone in on the roots where we wanted to see extra lift. In fact, it made our hair look so bouncy and full of body that our friends asked what styling products we used to get such a lush look. We also loved that it's fragrance-free so we could wear our perfume without having clashing scents.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Formula: There are many different ingredients that can make up a dry shampoo formula, but we favor those that are naturally-derived and provide a slew of benefits for your hair and scalp. For oil-absorbing properties, keep an eye out for formulas with arrowroot powder, tapioca starch, rice starch, and kaolin clay, as they will work wonders on keeping your greasy roots in check. We also love formulas with added hair-growth and strengthening benefits like biotin and rice protein, as they help keep your mane looking amazing in more ways that one. 
  • Volumizing Benefits: “Reducing oil at the root will always help maximize volume at the root,” notes Paula Peralta, hairstylist and Artistic Director at John Paul Mitchell Systems, while Giarrizzo explains further that “as your hair becomes dirtier in between shampoos, it becomes even flatter because it is weighed down with oil,” making a dry shampoo with volumizing benefits a must-have for those with fine hair. 
  • Scent: "A dirty scalp generally has an odor," says Giarrizzo. "So finding a dry shampoo that absorbs any odors and leaves a fresh-smelling scent is key to maintaining a clean feeling and scent.” 

Your Questions, Answered

What is the healthiest dry shampoo for your hair? 

Dry shampoos with natural, clean ingredients are stress-free options for fine hair types. Tapioca and corn starches, arrowroot powder, and kanolin clay attract grease and grime instantly without causing build-up on the scalp. Plus, the lack of alcohol in natural formulas prevents dehydration and potential irritation on the scalp. "Just remember that dry shampoo is not meant to replace a cleansing regimen," warns Peralta. "A good shampoo is the way to maintain a healthy scalp and hair."
