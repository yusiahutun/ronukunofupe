Woman is slammed for transforming vintage 1970s gown into a modern birthday dress

A woman who transformed a vintage 1970s gown into her dream birthday dress has sparked outrage online after being accused of cutting up a 'work of art' and turning it into 'something you'd find at Shein.'

Kelley Heyer, an actor in New York City, has more than 159,000 followers on TikTok, where she shares videos of her outfits, including her vintage finds and upcycled creations. 

She went viral this month after modeling a whimsical blue gown she bought for $55 on eBay, explaining in the caption that she was 'attempting to turn this already special vintage 70s dress into something even more special.'

'I'm a Pisces, so I really wanted to lean into some watery mermaid-core vibes, but I also wanted to keep the vintage feel of the dress,' she said. 

Kelley Heyer, an actor in New York City, went viral on TikTok after transforming a vintage dress from the 1970s into her dream birthday dress 

Heyer said she was drawn to the puffy sleeves and color when she bought the whimsical frock on eBay for $55 

Heyer, who took inspiration from vintage nightgowns and regency-era costumes, noted that she didn't want the dress to 'look like it was from Selkie.'

She started by cutting off the bottom of the dress to make it shorter and then reattaching the ruffle trim. She removed all of the floral lace with a seam ripper and replaced it with beading that she sewed on by hand. 

She had a friend help her lower the neckline, and she took the extra ruffle trim and attached it to the back of the dress to create a built-in train. 

Heyer's video has been viewed more than 1.4 million times, and hundreds of people flocked to the comments to share their thoughts — before she even had the chance to unveil her creation. 

A number of viewers were outraged that she cut the dress and removed the lace trim, insisting that she should have left it the way it was.  

'The original vintage piece is stunning. Would have loved to see it in the hands of someone who could appreciate and preserve it,' one person wrote. 

'NOOO THE DRESS WAS SO GORGEOUS,' another added.

She explained that when she got the dress in question in the mail, she could tell based on the stitches and construction that it was likely handmade. The fabric was also 'not particularly special or unique.' 

'My best guess is that before this dress came to me, it was either a theater costume or at the most a prom gown, and now I've turned it into my birthday dress,' she said.

When Heyer showed off the finished product in the next video, she asked viewers to be mindful of her feelings. 

'Please remember that this is my birthday dress and I'm really happy with the results,' she wrote in the caption. 

Heyer happily twirled around as she showed off her dress, saying, 'I love her so much. I feel like a mermaid. The beading reminds me of seafoam and bubbles, and the dress still feels vintage, which is exactly what I wanted.'

However, she once again faced backlash from commenters who slammed her for transforming the vintage dress, claiming her updates made it look like it was from off the rack.

Despite the criticism, there were also plenty of people who defended her choice and encouraged her to continue to upcycle vintage pieces. 

'Why does it matter if you liked the original dress?' one fan asked. 'She wanted to redo it and make it her own!! It's super sustainable.'

'Some of y'all are acting like I personally went into your home and burned your grandmother's wedding dress. Or like I took the original Betsy Ross flag and turned it into a Y2K crop top for Coachella,' she said.

'All of you are getting worked up over a dress that you didn't know existed until 24 hours ago,' she continued. 'I saw this dress on eBay...and I saw a vision for it. And I'm really happy with how it turned out. I think it looks so lovely and still has a vintage feel.'

She ended the clip by sharing screenshots of other vintage prairie gowns she found on eBay that were under $50.  

'Now you know these dresses are out there,' she concluded. 'So for those of you that are so obsessed with preserving history, you can buy these dresses yourself.'

The debate eventually carried over to Twitter, where user @lvamsgatthebeep slammed Heyer's critics. 

'A girl made a video where she got a 1970s dress & retooled it to be modern while still keeping the vintage feel & ppl are losing their gd minds. Bc she had the audacity to cut up a dress no one is wearing. The internet's obsession w preserving unused items as is is unhealthy,' she tweeted. 

'Everyone’s acting like this is some kind of sacred cultural artifact, & not another discarded unused item in a society obsessed with overconsumption where prices are rising and our landfills grow ever larger,' she added. 

After Heyer found out about the tweets in her defense, she joined Twitter to thank everyone for their support.   

'I had to make this account so I could say thank you for all the lovely comments!' she wrote. 'I had such a great time working on the piece and the result is exactly what I wanted.'
